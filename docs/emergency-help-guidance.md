# Emergency Help Guidance Principles

## Purpose

Germany Travel Checker may later include practical emergency help guidance for travelers in Germany, especially on weekends, Sundays and public holidays.

This content must be handled more carefully than regular travel guidance because it may involve health, safety or urgent needs.

The goal is to help travelers understand where to check official information, not to provide medical advice or fixed emergency provider listings.

## Allowed guidance

Germany Travel Checker may provide stable, general guidance such as:

- call 112 in a medical emergency or life-threatening situation
- call 110 for police emergencies
- use 116117 for non-emergency medical on-call help outside regular office hours
- regular pharmacies may be closed on Sundays and public holidays
- emergency pharmacies change by date and location
- travelers should check official emergency pharmacy search services, Google Maps or official local sources before going
- hotels, train stations, airports and local staff may help travelers find official local guidance

## Not allowed in MVP

The MVP must not provide:

- medical diagnosis
- treatment advice
- symptom triage
- advice to avoid medical care
- exact emergency room waiting times
- fixed emergency pharmacy listings
- fixed hospital availability
- exact weekend hospital recommendations
- exact emergency pharmacy or vending machine locations
- AI-generated emergency medical decisions

## Product safety rule

For medical emergencies, the product must clearly tell users to call 112 immediately.

For non-emergency medical concerns, the product may point users to 116117.

For pharmacies, the product may explain that regular pharmacies may be closed and that emergency pharmacies change by date and location.

The product should always ask travelers to verify current information using official local services, Google Maps or official business pages.

## Suggested English copy

### Emergency situation

If this is a medical emergency or a life-threatening situation, call 112 immediately.

### Non-emergency medical help

For non-emergency medical help outside regular office hours, travelers in Germany can check the medical on-call service at 116117. Availability, language support and local handling may vary.

### Emergency pharmacies

Regular pharmacies may be closed on Sundays and public holidays. Emergency pharmacies change by date and location, so always check an official Notdienst-Apotheke service, Google Maps or the official pharmacy website before going.

## Suggested Check Today placement

Emergency help should not be the first monetization feature.

It may be added later as one of these options:

- an expanded Pharmacy guidance card
- a separate Emergency help card
- a city survival guide section
- a weekend and public holiday help section

The first implementation should prefer a conservative Pharmacy guidance expansion before adding a separate emergency help mode.

## Data policy

Germany Travel Checker should not maintain its own emergency pharmacy database in the MVP.

If emergency pharmacy information is added later, it should link to official or authoritative lookup services instead of copying time-sensitive availability data.

Emergency provider data must not be generated by AI.

AI may later help explain official guidance in plain English, but it must not decide whether a traveler needs emergency care.
